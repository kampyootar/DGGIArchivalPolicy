BSA 2023

Accepts digital copies as original evidence if properly certified

The Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA) 2023 recognizes certified digital records as valid evidence, making digital archiving crucial for legal compliance.
  • Digital copies can be accepted as original evidence if properly certified.
  • The definition of "document" is broadened to include electronic and digital records, and the law explicitly recognizes electronic evidence.
  • Section 57: Electronic or digital records can be treated as primary evidence if proper custody and secure storage are ensured.
  • Section 61: The admissibility of evidence cannot be denied solely because it is electronic, provided there is compliance with Section 63 regarding procedures and requirements.

Practical Impact: DGGI must ensure digital records are securely stored and properly certified to be admissible in court under BSA 2023.

Key Compliance Steps:

  1. Maintain secure custody and access logs for all digital evidence.

  2. Ensure certification procedures comply with Section 63.

  3. Train staff on digital evidence handling and certification.

üíº

Electronic Evidence Under GST Law

Allows certified electronic records in GST cases

Section 145 of the CGST Act permits certified electronic records in GST-related cases, streamlining evidence submission and reducing paperwork.
  • Section 145(1): Recognizes microfilms, facsimiles, printouts, and electronically stored information as admissible documents.

  • Section 145(2): Requires certification describing the document, its creation, and device particulars for such electronic statements.

  • Allows admissibility of electronic/digital records without needing original production, upon valid certification.

BNSS 2023

Requires video recording of evidence collection and proper tracking

Section 145 of the CGST Act permits certified electronic records in GST-related cases, streamlining evidence submission and reducing paperwork.
  • BNSS 2023 provisions (Sections 3, 105, 176(3), 180(3), 193(2)(i), 254, 265, 266, 349) promote electronic handling of evidence and forensic rigor.
  • Mandates:
    • Forensic investigation and mandatory audio-video recording during search, seizure, or handling of digital exhibits.
    • Explicit requirement for maintaining chain of custody for electronic devices/evidence.

  • Ensures scientific rigor, integrity, and transparency in criminal investigations for admissibility in court.
